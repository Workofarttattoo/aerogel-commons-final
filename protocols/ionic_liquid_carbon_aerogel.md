# Ionic Liquid Templated Carbon Aerogel Synthesis Protocol

## Overview
This protocol describes the synthesis of carbon aerogels using ionic liquids as structure-directing agents, offering tunable porosity and high electrochemical performance.

## Materials Required

### Precursors
- Resorcinol - 10g
- Formaldehyde (37% solution) - 15mL
- 1-Ethyl-3-methylimidazolium tetrafluoroborate ([EMIM][BF₄]) - 20g
- Sodium carbonate (catalyst) - 0.1g
- Distilled water - 50mL

### Equipment
- Magnetic stirrer hotplate
- Vacuum oven
- Tube furnace (up to 1000°C)
- Supercritical CO₂ dryer
- pH meter
- Analytical balance

## Safety Considerations
- Formaldehyde is toxic and carcinogenic - handle in fume hood
- Ionic liquids can be irritating - wear gloves
- High temperature carbonization requires heat-resistant PPE
- CO₂ system requires pressure training

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### Step 1: Precursor Solution Preparation (30 minutes)
1. Dissolve resorcinol (10g) in distilled water (20mL)
2. Add ionic liquid ([EMIM][BF₄], 20g) and mix until homogeneous
3. Add formaldehyde solution (15mL) dropwise with stirring
4. Add sodium carbonate catalyst (0.1g)
5. Adjust total volume to 50mL with distilled water

**Molar Ratios**:
- Resorcinol:Formaldehyde = 1:2
- Ionic liquid:Resorcinol = 2:1 (by weight)

### Step 2: Gelation and Curing (48 hours)
1. Stir solution at room temperature for 30 minutes
2. Transfer to glass molds or vials
3. Cure at 85°C for 24 hours (sealed containers)
4. Age at room temperature for additional 24 hours

**Reaction**:
```
Resorcinol + Formaldehyde → Cross-linked polymer gel (ionic liquid templated)
```

### Step 3: Solvent Exchange (24 hours)
1. Replace aqueous phase with acetone (3 exchanges, 8 hours each)
2. Final exchange with liquid CO₂
3. Ensure complete removal of water and ionic liquid

### Step 4: Supercritical Drying (4 hours)
1. Load samples into supercritical dryer
2. Exchange with liquid CO₂ (3 cycles)
3. Heat to 40°C, pressure 1200 psi
4. Depressurize slowly over 2 hours

**Result**: Mesoporous organic aerogel with ionic liquid template

### Step 5: Carbonization (4 hours)
1. Transfer dried aerogel to tube furnace
2. Purge with nitrogen gas for 30 minutes
3. Heat to 800°C at 5°C/min
4. Hold at 800°C for 2 hours
5. Cool slowly to room temperature under N₂

**Chemical Changes**:
- Polymer → Amorphous carbon
- Ionic liquid decomposition and removal
- Pore structure preservation

### Step 6: Activation (Optional, 2 hours)
1. Heat carbon aerogel to 800°C under CO₂ atmosphere
2. React for 1-2 hours:
   ```
   C + CO₂ → 2CO (gasification)
   ```
3. Increases surface area and microporosity

## Characterization

### Physical Properties
- **Density**: 0.2-0.5 g/cm³
- **Surface Area**: 800-2000 m²/g (after activation)
- **Pore Volume**: 1.0-2.5 cm³/g
- **Average Pore Size**: 2-10 nm

### Electrochemical Properties
- **Electrical Conductivity**: 1-10 S/cm
- **Capacitance**: 150-300 F/g
- **Cyclic Stability**: >95% after 1000 cycles
- **Rate Capability**: 80% capacity retention at high rates

### Structural Analysis
- **Raman**: D/G band ratio indicates graphitization degree
- **XRD**: Amorphous carbon structure
- **SEM/TEM**: Hierarchical pore structure
- **XPS**: Surface functional groups

## Process Optimization

### Ionic Liquid Selection
- **[EMIM][BF₄]**: Balanced hydrophobicity, good templating
- **[BMIM][PF₆]**: Larger pores, higher thermal stability
- **[HMIM][Tf₂N]**: Enhanced conductivity, wider electrochemical window

### Pore Structure Control
- **Ionic Liquid Concentration**: Higher IL → larger pores
- **Carbonization Temperature**: 600-1000°C affects pore size
- **Activation Time**: Longer activation → higher surface area

## Expected Results
- **Appearance**: Black, lightweight monoliths
- **Recovery**: 40-60% carbon yield from precursors
- **Surface Area**: 1000-1500 m²/g (typical)
- **Conductivity**: 2-5 S/cm
- **Pore Size Distribution**: Mesoporous (2-50 nm)

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues
- **No Gelation**: Check formaldehyde concentration, ensure proper mixing
- **Cracked Monoliths**: Slow drying, incomplete solvent exchange
- **Low Surface Area**: Incomplete template removal, optimize carbonization
- **Poor Conductivity**: Insufficient carbonization temperature

### Ionic Liquid Issues
- **Template Removal**: Ensure complete decomposition during carbonization
- **Phase Separation**: Optimize ionic liquid/water ratios
- **Cost**: Ionic liquids expensive - consider recycling

## Scale-Up Considerations
