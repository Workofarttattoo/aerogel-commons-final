## Overview
This protocol describes the biological synthesis of alginate-based aerogels using engineered Pseudomonas aeruginosa bacteria, offering a sustainable and biocompatible alternative to synthetic polymers.

### Step 2: Fermentation Culture (48 hours)
1. Inoculate 500mL LB broth (antibiotic-free) with 1% overnight culture
2. Incubate at 30¬įC, 150 rpm for 48 hours
3. Monitor growth (OD600 should reach 8-10)

### Step 3: Alginate Extraction (4 hours)
1. Resuspend cell pellet in 100mL distilled water
2. Add 10mL 1M NaOH, stir 30 min at 60¬įC
3. Centrifuge to remove cell debris

**Yield**: 2-5g alginate per liter culture
**Molecular Weight**: 100-200 kDa (lower than seaweed alginate)

### Step 4: Hydrogel Formation (2 hours)
1. Prepare 1% alginate solution in distilled water
2. Degas solution under vacuum (30 min)
3. Add calcium chloride solution dropwise (0.1M final concentration)

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues
- **No gelation**: Check calcium concentration (0.05-0.2M optimal)
- **Weak gels**: Increase alginate concentration or molecular weight
- **Contamination**: Strict aseptic technique required
- **Low yield**: Optimize fermentation conditions (pH 7.0, 30¬įC)
